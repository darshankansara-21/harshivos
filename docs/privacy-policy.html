Privacy Policy

HARSHIVOS Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2 July 2026

HARSHIVOS ("the app", "we", "us") is a sensory and life-skills companion designed for autistic children, children with disabilities, and their caregivers. We built this app for our own child first, and we treat your child's privacy the way we treat his: with the greatest care.

The short version. HARSHIVOS is offline-first. It does not require an account. It does not show ads. It does not use analytics or tracking. It does not sell or share personal information. Information you enter stays on your device.

1. Who this app is for

HARSHIVOS is intended to be used by children under the supervision of a parent, caregiver, therapist, or teacher. Because it is directed to children, we comply with the principles of the U.S. Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and the child-privacy provisions of the EU/UK GDPR.

2. Information the app handles

HARSHIVOS is designed to collect as little as possible. The only information involved is data a caregiver or child chooses to enter for the app to work, which is stored locally on the device:

  • A child's first name or nickname (optional) — used only to personalize greetings on the device.
  • Progress data — completed routines, streaks, favorites, and an "independence score." Stored on the device.
  • Personalization — the child's chosen avatar appearance and any custom routines a caregiver creates. Stored on the device.

We do not collect real names, addresses, email addresses, phone numbers, precise location, contacts, photos, microphone audio, or persistent advertising identifiers.

3. Where the data is stored

All of the above is stored in the app's own local storage on your device. It is not transmitted to us or to any third party by default. If you uninstall the app, this local data is removed by the operating system.

4. Optional cloud sync

The app is built to optionally support cloud backup in the future so a child's progress can move between their own devices. This feature is disabled by default and performs no data transfer unless a caregiver explicitly enables and configures it in a future version. If and when it is enabled, this policy will be updated to describe exactly what is stored and where.

5. Optional AI features

Some features (such as generating a custom routine or a social story) can optionally use an AI service. By default the app uses an on-device, offline generator and sends nothing externally. A caregiver may choose to configure an external AI provider (for example, a locally-run model, or a third-party service using the caregiver's own API key). If a caregiver does so, the text of that specific request is sent to the provider they configured, governed by that provider's privacy policy. We recommend caregivers never enter a child's real name or identifying details into AI prompts.

6. Voice / text-to-speech

The app uses your device's built-in text-to-speech to read instructions aloud. This uses the operating system's own speech engine and its privacy terms. The app does not record or listen to the microphone.

7. No advertising or analytics

HARSHIVOS contains no third-party advertising SDKs, no analytics SDKs, and no social-media trackers.

8. Data sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share personal information with third parties. Because personal data stays on the device, we generally have no access to it.

9. Parental rights & control

Because data is stored locally and no account exists, a parent or caregiver is always in full control. You can review or delete the child's information at any time by clearing it in the app or uninstalling the app from the device. If you have questions about a child's data, contact us using the details below.

10. Security

We rely on the device's operating-system protections to keep local app data private. No method of storage is 100% secure, but keeping data on-device and off our servers substantially reduces risk.

11. Changes to this policy

If we change this policy, we will update the "Last updated" date above and publish the new version at this same web address. Material changes affecting data practices will be reflected in a future app update.
